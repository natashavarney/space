Design inquiry into Urban Nature

Today we seem to be have forgotten to use our senses and to see the beauty of the nature that is all around us. Through an embodied design practices, I have used Sense-ethnography and Walkshops to open a design field of nuances, refinement and everyday observations of reflection to amplify the need to revise our understanding of the senses and how nature influence every aspect of our day to day experiences.
Since the inquiry is a bit experimental i wanted the methods for the designprocess to mirror the same experimental mind-set. I use Emplaced Ethnography, Unexpected, Walkshops, Emotional Biosensing & Walking with Video to capture my data and Clustering & Affinity Diagramming to make sense of the many insights gained.
I also experimented with using portable digital technologies such as smart phones, heart rate sensors and Galvanic Skin Response sensors (GSR) to have an alternative inquiry into peoples perception of the urban nature and their connection to it.
I am still precieding my investigation and collaboration with Growing Pathways to look into what the future of sensing our beautiful urban nature brings and how design can contribute to this.
